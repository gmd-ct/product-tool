# Research Methodology

## Overview

This research was conducted through systematic web research across multiple source types to build a comprehensive understanding of each product management tool.

## Source Categories

### Primary Sources
- **Official Websites**: Product pages, feature lists, pricing pages
- **Documentation**: Help centers, API docs, guides
- **Company Content**: Blog posts, case studies, webinars

### Secondary Sources
- **Review Platforms**: G2, Capterra, TrustRadius
- **Community**: Reddit, Twitter/X, Product Hunt
- **Analyst Content**: Comparison articles, industry reports

### Tertiary Sources
- **User Content**: YouTube tutorials, blog reviews
- **News**: Funding announcements, acquisitions
- **Job Postings**: Tech stack, team structure signals

## Research Framework Per Tool

### 1. Company Intelligence
- Founding story and leadership
- Funding and growth trajectory
- Market positioning

### 2. Feature Inventory
Complete mapping across categories:
- Roadmapping
- Prioritization
- Idea Management
- Strategy & OKRs
- Collaboration
- Integrations
- Reporting
- AI/Automation
- Admin & Security

### 3. Pricing Analysis
- All tier breakdowns
- Feature limits per tier
- Hidden costs and considerations

### 4. User Research
- Target personas
- Use case patterns
- Workflow examples
- Scale considerations

### 5. Sentiment Analysis
- Review theme extraction
- Common praise patterns
- Common complaint patterns
- NPS/satisfaction signals

### 6. Competitive Position
- Differentiation claims
- Marketing messaging
- Positioning strategy

## Data Collection

- 20-30 web searches per tool
- Multiple source triangulation
- Structured data extraction
- Qualitative insight synthesis

## Limitations

- Pricing may change; verified as of research date
- Enterprise pricing often requires sales contact
- Some features may be in beta or limited availability
- User sentiment represents sample, not full population
