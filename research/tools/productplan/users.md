# ProductPlan - Users, Personas & Use Cases

## Target Users

### Primary Personas

#### 1. Product Managers
**Role**: Core users who build and maintain roadmaps
- Create and update product roadmaps
- Prioritize features and initiatives
- Communicate strategy to stakeholders
- Link work to business objectives

**Why They Choose ProductPlan**:
- Intuitive interface reduces time spent on tool management
- Visual roadmaps easy to share with non-technical stakeholders
- Quick to learn, quick to create first roadmap

#### 2. Product Leaders / Directors / VPs
**Role**: Strategic oversight and portfolio management
- View portfolio of roadmaps across teams
- Ensure alignment with company objectives
- Present to executives and board
- Manage resource allocation across initiatives

**Why They Choose ProductPlan**:
- Master Plan view aggregates multiple roadmaps
- Board-friendly export formats
- Executive-level visualizations
- OKR alignment features

#### 3. Executives / C-Suite
**Role**: Strategic decision-makers
- Review high-level roadmaps
- Approve major initiatives
- Understand product direction
- Track progress against goals

**Why They Choose ProductPlan**:
- Clean, professional presentations
- High-level strategic views
- Easy to understand without training

#### 4. Cross-Functional Stakeholders
**Role**: Contributors and consumers of roadmap information
- Engineering leads
- Marketing teams
- Sales teams
- Customer success
- Design teams

**Why They Choose ProductPlan**:
- Unlimited viewer access (free)
- View-only links easy to share
- Commenting for feedback
- Embedded in Confluence/wikis

## Use Case Examples

### Use Case 1: Visual Roadmap Communication

**Scenario**: Product team needs to communicate quarterly roadmap to entire organization

**Workflow**:
1. PM creates roadmap with timeline view
2. Color-codes by theme/initiative type
3. Adds milestones for key dates
4. Generates view-only link
5. Shares via Slack/email
6. Embeds in Confluence for reference
7. Exports PDF for board presentation

**Value**: "ProductPlan gives us a clear, visual way to organize our initiatives and helps connect strategy, timelines, and ownership in one place."

### Use Case 2: Multi-Product Portfolio Management

**Scenario**: Product leader managing roadmaps across 5 product lines

**Workflow**:
1. Each product team maintains their roadmap
2. Leader uses Master Plan to aggregate views
3. Portfolio view shows cross-team dependencies
4. Strategic alignment with company OKRs
5. Board-level reporting from single view

**Value**: "Being able to share a view only version of roadmaps to non-users and embed in wiki pages is a huge plus."

### Use Case 3: Prioritization and Planning

**Scenario**: Team needs to prioritize Q2 initiatives from large backlog

**Workflow**:
1. Capture all ideas in parking lot
2. Use prioritization board for scoring
3. Apply RICE or custom framework
4. Score and stack rank initiatives
5. Drag top priorities to roadmap
6. Link to OKRs for justification

**Value**: "ProductPlan allows for prioritization of work within a fiscal year, ensuring transparency and alignment among teams."

### Use Case 4: Jira Integration for Dev Alignment

**Scenario**: PM needs to keep roadmap synced with engineering sprints

**Workflow**:
1. Connect ProductPlan to Jira
2. Import epics as roadmap containers
3. Enable two-way sync
4. Status updates flow automatically
5. Dependencies synced between systems
6. Single source of truth maintained

**Value**: "The Jira integration adds so much value, giving you up-to-date status on the implementation/engineering effort."

### Use Case 5: Business Case Development

**Scenario**: PM building cases for potential new features

**Workflow**:
1. Capture opportunities in idea capture
2. Add context and supporting data
3. Score against strategic criteria
4. Use planning board for discussion
5. Move approved ideas to roadmap
6. Track from idea to delivery

**Value**: "Building out business cases for potential projects, prioritizing those projects, and then transparency and traceability into executing those projects."

## Team Structures That Work Well

### Centralized Product Team
- Single product org with multiple PMs
- Shared roadmap templates
- Consistent prioritization frameworks
- Portfolio view for leadership

### Distributed Product Teams
- Multiple product lines
- Each team owns their roadmap
- Master Plan for aggregation
- Consistent look and feel

### Product + Engineering Alignment
- PMs own roadmap strategy
- Engineering visibility via Jira sync
- Shared timeline understanding
- Reduced status meetings
