## Company History

### Founding Story
Airfocus was founded in 2017 in Hamburg, Germany by Malte Scholz, Christian Hoffmeister, and Valentin Firak. The founders aimed to solve common product management challenges with a new approach: modularity.

The company positioned itself as "the world's first modular product management tool," differentiating from monolithic competitors like Aha! and Productboard with a flexible, customizable approach.

### Key Milestones
- **2017**: Company founded in Hamburg
- **November 2019**: First funding round (Seed)
- **June 2021**: Series A ($5M)
- **January 2024**: Series A extension ($7.5M)
- **April 2025**: Acquired by Lucid Software

## Product Overview

### The Modular Approach
Airfocus is "the market's first and only modular product management platform." Unlike competitors with fixed structures, airfocus allows teams to:
- Customize every aspect of their workspace
- Build workflows that match existing processes
- Scale without forced standardization

### Core Capabilities
1. **Roadmaps** - Tailored roadmaps for products and teams
2. **Prioritization** - Priority Poker and scoring frameworks
3. **Feedback & Insights** - Centralized customer feedback
4. **OKRs & Objectives** - Goal alignment
5. **Portfolio Management** - Multi-product oversight
6. **Capacity Planning** - Resource and scenario planning
